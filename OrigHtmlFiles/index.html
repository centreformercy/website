Our Ministry

SUNDAY BULLETIN

SUNDAY, MARCH 4 2ND SUNDAY OF LENT

Jesus took Peter, James, and John up a mountain to pray (Luke 9,28b)

Today I have the privilege of giving the homily at St. Charles Borromeo parish in honor of my brother Don's 40th anniversary celebration of his priesthood.  This is a good perspective of a "mountain top experience" as I look back on his 40 years of priesthood. 

I also have just finished giving a parish mission at Blessed Trinity parish in Brusselss Illinois.  This also gives me a good point for a special perspective on my life and for looking toward the rest of Lent.

On the first night of the mission I talked about the importance of living out of a vision for our life and renewing that vision on a daily basis.  Each day or season that vision gets concretized according to the way God is guiding us through the Holy Spirit to focus on our spiritual journety at that moment.

My brother Don's anniversary reminds me of the words in the homily of his first Mass by Father Bernard Huhn, his spiritual director at the time, when he said "Fr. Don may you never forget who yor are, a priest of God."  I can see all the ways that he has integrated his priesthood into his personality and how at the time of my brother, Tom's dying and death last November, he especially was present to him as a loving big brother who also tended to his soul.  That serves as an example for me to want to live that grace and to be formed more deeply into it this Lent.

At the parish mission I seemed to discover and have affirmed in one of my personal conferences that the needy to whom I especially am called to minister at this time of my life are those struggling to find joy in the midst of their trials. I hope that the development of this website can speak to that need in both English and Spanish.  My "mountain top" vision at the beginning of this year is to "leap from my security into the arms of the needy" and to approach my "new born" ministry with the care and attention of my 41 year old friends who have just learned of an unexpected pregnancy and need to prepare themselves for another twenty years of nurturing and forming this new child to adulthood.

We all ask ourselves this Sunday, "what is the vision that you have for me this Lent, Lord?"
